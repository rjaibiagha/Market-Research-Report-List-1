<p><p>The wireless tower market comprises various structures designed for telecommunications. Three tube wireless towers feature a triangular design for stability and ease of installation, ideal for urban settings. Angle-steel wireless towers, constructed from steel angles, provide robust support and are commonly used in rural areas for extended reach. Guyed wireless towers rely on external cables for stability, making them suitable for taller structures and harsh environments, offering significant height advantage at a lower material cost, which is critical for expansive coverage.</p></p>
