<p><p>Whole Slide Imaging (WSI) scanners are used for digitizing histopathological slides in two main types: Brightfield and Fluorescence. Brightfield imaging captures traditional stained tissue samples, providing clear, detailed images of cellular structures under standard illumination. In contrast, Fluorescence imaging uses specific wavelengths of light to excite fluorescent markers applied to samples, enabling visualization of particular cell components or proteins. Both types are essential for digital pathology, facilitating diagnosis, research, and education through enhanced image analysis capabilities.</p></p>
