Metal conduits, typically made from materials like steel or aluminum, provide robust mechanical protection and are resistant to environmental factors, making them suitable for industrial applications. Non-metallic conduits, made from materials such as PVC or polyethylene, offer flexibility, corrosion resistance, and lightweight properties, making them ideal for residential and commercial installations. Both types serve to protect electrical cables from damage and ensure safety.</p></p>
